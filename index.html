Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about RC vehicles and our services

For beginners, we recommend starting with a Ready-to-Run (RTR) model that's durable and easy to maintain. The Traxxas Slash 2WD, Arrma Granite, and ECX Torment are excellent options that balance performance, durability, and affordability. These vehicles are user-friendly and have widely available replacement parts.
Regular maintenance depends on how frequently you use your RC. After each run, clean off dirt and debris, check for loose screws, and inspect for damage. Every 5-10 runs, perform more thorough maintenance including gear mesh inspection, bearing lubrication, and shock inspection. For racers or heavy users, we recommend a complete overhaul every 3-6 months.
The most effective performance upgrades typically include: 1) Better electronics (motor, ESC, and LiPo batteries), 2) Quality suspension components (shocks, springs), 3) Lightweight drivetrain parts, and 4) Improved tires suited to your driving surface. The best approach is to upgrade strategically based on your specific goals - whether that's speed, handling, durability, or runtime.
Yes, our skilled technicians can repair and service virtually all RC brands and models, including Traxxas, Arrma, Losi, Axial, HPI, Tamiya, and many more. We have extensive experience with electric and nitro vehicles, from entry-level models to high-end racing machines. Our shop is equipped with specialized tools and diagnostic equipment to handle all types of repairs.
Brushed motors use physical brushes to transfer power, making them more affordable but less efficient with shorter lifespans. Brushless motors have electronic commutation, offering higher efficiency, more power, longer runtime, and extended lifespan. While brushless systems cost more initially, they deliver superior performance and better long-term value for enthusiasts looking for more speed and runtime.
